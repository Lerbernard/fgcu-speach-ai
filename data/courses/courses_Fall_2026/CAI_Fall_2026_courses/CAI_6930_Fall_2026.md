Note: Introduces the fundamental concepts, technologies, and challenges of autonomous driving systems, including vehicle architecture, perception, localization and mapping, motion planning, control, and system integration, along with afety, ethical, and regulatory considerations. Through lectures, hands-on labs, and a semester project, students gain practical experience using autonomous driving software frameworks and simulation tools. Students engage in research-oriented analysis of advanced topics such as algorithm design, multimodal sensor fusion, object detection and tracking, SLAM, trajectory planning, vehicle control, simulation-based validation, quantitative performance evaluation, and safety assurance for real-world autonomous systems
