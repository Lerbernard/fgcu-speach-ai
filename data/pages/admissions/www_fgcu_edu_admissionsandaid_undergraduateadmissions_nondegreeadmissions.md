# Non-Degree Admissions

## Florida Residency for Tuition — Special Categories
The following individuals may qualify for in-state tuition under special categories:
1. A student who was enrolled as a Florida resident for tuition purposes at a Florida public college/university, abandoned Florida domicile, and then re-enrolled in Florida within 12 months of the abandonment, provided they continuously maintain the re-established domicile in Florida during enrollment. (This benefit applies one time only.)
2. An active duty member of the U.S. Armed Services residing or stationed in Florida (and spouse/dependent student), and an active drilling member of the Florida National Guard at the time of acceptance; or military personnel not stationed in Florida whose home of record or state of legal residence (DD Form 2058) is Florida (and spouse/dependent student).
3. An active duty member of the U.S. Armed Services (and spouse/dependent student) attending a public college or university within 50 miles of the military establishment where stationed, if that establishment is within a county contiguous to Florida.
4. Full-time instructional and administrative personnel employed by the State of Florida public school system or a Florida public college/university (and spouse/dependent children).
5. A student from Latin America and the Caribbean who receives scholarships from the federal or state government, attending a Florida public college/university full-time.
6. A full-time employee of a state agency or political subdivision of the state, when student fees are paid by the agency for job-related law enforcement or corrections training.
7. An active duty member of the Canadian military residing or stationed in Florida under the NORAD agreement (and spouse/dependent student) attending a public college or university within 50 miles of the military establishment where stationed.
8. An active duty member of a foreign nation's military serving as a liaison officer, residing or stationed in Florida (and spouse/dependent student), attending a public college or university within 50 miles of the establishment where stationed.
9. A qualified beneficiary under the Stanley G. Tate Florida Pre-Paid College Program.
10. A Linkage Institute participant receiving partial or full exemptions per Florida statute.
11. Military personnel (and spouse/dependent children) not stationed in Florida but whose home of record or state of legal residence is Florida (per DD Form 2058, or a Leave and Earning Statement / DFAS Form 702).

## Immigration & International Student Issues
A permanent resident has been granted permanent resident status in the U.S. and has (or is waiting for) a Permanent Resident Card (Form I-151 or Form I-551), and is eligible to establish residency for tuition purposes in Florida. A non-immigrant is a foreign national seeking to enter the U.S. temporarily for a specific purpose. A student with certain non-immigrant visas may be eligible to establish residency for tuition purposes — contact the Admissions office for more information. An unauthorized alien is not eligible to establish residency for tuition purposes in Florida; however, a dependent student who is a U.S. citizen may not be denied resident classification based solely on the immigration status of their parents.

## Specialized Admissions Programs
A specialized admissions program is one where both program admission and registration in program classes are restricted to a certain number of students meeting pre-determined criteria. Students are initially accepted into the university as a pre-major and then must submit a supplemental application for acceptance into the specialized admissions major. This status applies where student demand exceeds available resources or where students require minimum skills to be admitted (such as Music or PGA Golf Management). The selection process is competitive and space is limited. Freshmen do not submit supplemental applications; applications are due in the sophomore year or when program prerequisite requirements have been completed.

## Foreign Language Requirements
All students admitted as freshmen or transfers must satisfy the foreign language admission requirement. A limited number of undergraduate students not meeting this requirement may be admitted, but must fulfill it prior to completing the baccalaureate degree. Competency can be demonstrated by:
1. Completing a foreign language or American Sign Language course in high school at the 2nd-year level or higher (e.g., Spanish 2, Haitian Creole 2, American Sign Language 2).
2. Completing a foreign language college course at the elementary 2 level or higher.
3. Obtaining qualifying scores on the AP, AICE, IB, and/or CLEP exams (see the FGCU Academic Catalog for qualifying scores).
