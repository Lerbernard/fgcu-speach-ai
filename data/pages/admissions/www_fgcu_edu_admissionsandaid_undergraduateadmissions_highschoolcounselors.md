# Information for High School Counselors

Undergraduate Admissions provides current information and resources to help counselors guide students toward a smooth transition from high school to FGCU.

## Admission Competitiveness
To be competitive for admission, students must have a 3.0 weighted GPA or higher with 500 or higher on each of the rSAT sections and a 1070 or higher composite, and/or 19s or higher on the ACT Reading, Math, and English sub-sections with a 21 or higher composite. Meeting these minimums does not guarantee admission. Students with lower subsection scores should plan to retest.

Admission averages for 2018–2019:
- 3.87 weighted GPA
- Composite scores: 23.4 ACT and/or 1141 SAT
- Students who apply Early Action (by November 1) are given priority consideration for merit awards
- The Regular Decision deadline for Summer and Fall is March 1

## Dates, Reminders and Next Steps

**Eagle View Orientation**
Participation is mandatory for all new FGCU students in order to register for classes.

**Housing**
Students may only apply for housing through Gulfline after being admitted.

**Residency**
Residency information is processed for admitted students when possible. Residency information is not needed to complete an application or make an admission decision; the top priority is processing application documents first to make a timely decision.

**Gulfline PIN**
After being admitted, students are emailed a personal identification number (PIN) to log in to Gulfline, where they set up two-factor authentication. (Check junk mail if it does not appear by the evening of admission day.) PINs are not provided via fax or telephone; requests must be made in person or in writing to the Records & Registration office.
