# Transfer Admissions

FGCU is transfer friendly for students who have earned 12 or more college credits:
- Easygoing admission requirements
- Guaranteed admission with an Associate in Arts (AA) degree
- Test scores optional with 30+ transferable college credits (including college-level Math & English)
- Personalized and flexible credit transfer process
- Affordable tuition
- Generous scholarship and financial aid resources

Connect with the Transfer Admissions Team at (239) 590-1111, request more info, or schedule a transfer tour. The Transfer Tool in Degree Works shows how your classes might transfer to FGCU.

## Who is a Transfer Applicant?
FGCU considers you a transfer applicant if you have a high school diploma from a regionally accredited high school or GED and have completed at least 12 semester hours of transferable college credit after graduation from high school, but have not completed a bachelor's degree.

High school students taking dual-enrollment courses should apply using the freshman (first time in college) application, unless they plan to complete 12 or more semester hours of college coursework after graduating from high school. College coursework will still be considered for transfer credit, so college transcripts should be sent to the Office of Undergraduate Admissions.

You are a second bachelor's degree applicant if you have a bachelor's degree from a regionally accredited college or university (including FGCU). DACA and Undocumented students should refer to the FGCU DACA & Undocumented FAQ and Non-Resident Tuition waiver.

## Transferring from a Partner College (FSW, SCF, or SFSC)
Destination FGCU provides students from partner colleges with the tools and guidance needed to easily and successfully transfer to FGCU to complete a bachelor's degree.

## General Requirements Applicable to All Transfer Applicants
1. Transfer applicants have earned 12 or more transferable college credits since receiving a standard high school diploma or its equivalent. Undergraduate Admissions determines the number of transferable credits by evaluating college transcripts. Applicants who earn transferable credits before graduating high school are considered freshmen; if they earn 12 or more credits after high school graduation, they are transfer applicants. Applicants are classified as upper-level, mid-level, or lower-level transfer.
2. Academic status must be in good standing and the applicant must be eligible to return to the last institution attended as a degree-seeking student.
3. Meeting minimum admission requirements does not guarantee admission into FGCU or its degree programs. Individual programs may have additional requirements including a supplemental application, minimum GPA, prerequisite courses, and test scores. Applications received or completed after posted deadlines may be processed for the requested term based on space or time limitations, or offered admission into the next available term.
4. The following types of college courses are not transferable: technical, vocational, physical education, remedial courses, or courses for which a grade below D- is received.
5. Must satisfy the language requirement.

An evaluation of your application may take up to two weeks after your file is completed, and longer if the review is on hold.

## Process and Transcripts
1. Submit the FGCU Undergraduate Admission Application online, answer the conduct questions, and provide the necessary documentation.
2. Send your official high school transcript reflecting all completed coursework and a graduation date. Florida public high schools can send transcripts electronically through FASTER. Transcripts sent from a personal email address or by fax are unofficial and will not be accepted. Applicants with a GED must submit official GED test scores; home-schooled applicants must submit a properly formatted transcript; applicants with an international high school transcript must submit a transcript evaluated by a credential evaluation agency.
3. Send official college transcripts from all colleges and/or universities previously attended to the Undergraduate Admissions office.
