# International Student Admissions

The admissions process for international students begins with International Admissions. This office provides university information and applications, determines admission acceptance (including issuing I-20s), and works with Global Engagement to prepare international students for enrollment. International students who meet all university and U.S. Immigration requirements may be accepted.

You should begin six months to one year prior to the date of beginning classes to complete required academic admissions tests, obtain immunizations, secure financial support, and prepare other required documents. The application and all official supporting documents must be submitted by the published deadlines for the requested entry date. This school is authorized under Federal law to enroll non-immigrant alien students.

## Education in the United States
Students who enter a U.S. university must complete 12 years (or equivalent) of preparatory education. International students applying as first-time-in-college freshmen must have the equivalent of a U.S. high school diploma.

FGCU does not evaluate foreign credentials or transcripts on campus. For international student transcript evaluations, contact an approved international evaluation agency. A course-by-course evaluation is required for college courses taken and is recommended for high school equivalency unless a GPA is included in the evaluation (in which case a document-by-document evaluation will suffice). Request that the agency send the results directly by mail to Undergraduate Admissions as an official document. For additional information, contact the Admissions Office toll-free at (888) 889-1095.

University-level coursework undertaken abroad may be transferable if earned at a recognized institution, depending on academic equivalency. Students interested in transferring academic credit should submit an original evaluation of courses taken and an English translation of course syllabi for which credit is desired. Academic departments may deny credit for any coursework not applicable to that department. The University recommends the course-by-course evaluation, with grades or marks and credit hours equated to the U.S. system. First-time-in-college or lower-level transfer students may choose the document-by-document evaluation for high-school courses; if the agency does not provide a GPA, the student must meet the minimum ACT (21) or SAT (1060) requirement. Transcripts and test scores received and opened by the university become the sole property of FGCU and may not be transferred to a third party or the applicant.

Applicants are classified as international if they are not U.S. citizens, dual citizens, or permanent residents, and if they are applying for an F-1 student visa or a J-1 Exchange Visitor visa. International students may apply to any degree program or major for which the University has been granted authorization to enroll international students.

## Admission Criteria
FGCU requires applicants to submit official English language proficiency test scores if English is not the native language. Language proficiency may be met by attending a US high school for 4 years and graduating, or earning a degree from a US college or university, or by meeting minimum test scores:
- TOEFL Computerized test: minimum 213
- TOEFL Paper-based test: minimum 550
- TOEFL Internet-based test: minimum 79
- IELTS: minimum 6.5
- Duolingo: minimum 110

FGCU reviews test scores and transcripts to determine admissibility; meeting the minimum requirements does not guarantee admission. Note: admission deadlines for international applicants differ from those for domestic applicants.

## Visa Information and I-20 Forms
The Office of Admissions issues the I-20 Form (the certificate of eligibility to study in the U.S.) to international students who intend to pursue full-time studies to obtain an undergraduate or graduate degree. An I-20 will not be issued to any student who does not meet university admission and U.S. immigration requirements. International students must present the I-20 to a U.S. Embassy or Consulate when applying for a student visa (generally an F-1 visa). If you currently have a U.S. visa and are in the U.S., you may be required to complete a Visa Clearance Form or submit additional immigration forms, and may need to present your I-94 Card, passport, U.S. visa, and other immigration documents to the Office of Admissions.

If you currently have a visa other than an F-1, contact the Office of Admissions to discuss your immigration status; each visa category has restrictions on enrollment. Prospective students with expired immigration documents may need an immigration attorney. FGCU does not provide legal advice on immigration matters. Prospective students determined to be "out of status" with immigration cannot be admitted.

## Immunization Record
All international students must provide proof of vaccination against Rubella and Measles (MMR) before registering for classes. Effective July 1, 2008, all new matriculating students must provide documentation of vaccinations against meningococcal meningitis and hepatitis B, or provide a signed waiver for each declined vaccination, in addition to the MMR requirement. A student cannot waive the MMR requirement. For more information, visit Student Health Services.

The International Services Office provides support for admitted international students, including orientation to living and studying in the U.S., assistance with cross-cultural adjustment, and help with U.S. immigration documents and regulations.
