# Environmental Engineering B.S.Env.E. — Degree Map

## Program Overview
The Bachelor of Science in Environmental Engineering at FGCU is ABET-accredited. Environmental engineers apply principles of physics, chemistry, and biology to protect, restore, and sustain human health and critical ecosystems including water, air, and land resources. Total credits required: 122.

Average salary for FGCU environmental engineering alumni: $76,912.
72% of graduates stay in Florida.

## Graduation Requirements
- Complete a minimum of 122 credits
- At least 42 upper division credits (3000-4999 level)
- Cumulative GPA of 2.0 at FGCU
- Minimum grade of C in each required course
- Satisfy Service-Learning, Civic Literacy, and Sustainability requirements
- ENV 4612C satisfies the Sustainability Course Graduation Requirement

## Combined B.S.Env.E. / M.S. Program

Qualified students can complete both the B.S.Env.E. and M.S. in Civil Engineering in approximately 5 years.

**Admission Requirements:**
- Junior standing or higher
- Major GPA of at least 3.5
- Overall GPA of at least 3.2

**Program Details:**
- B.S.Env.E. is 122 credits, M.S. is 30 credits
- Up to 9 graduate credits double-counted for both degrees
- Total combined program: 143 credit hours
- Minimum grade of B- required for each double-counted graduate course

**Approved graduate courses for double-counting include:**
- CWR 6532 Urban Water Modeling
- CWR 6537 Contaminant Transport
- CWR 6637 Storm Water Management and Modeling
- EGN 6430 Advanced Engineering Analysis
- EGN 6457 Research Methods
- EGN 6730 Renewable Sustainable Systems
- ENV 6516 Sustainable Water Quality Engineering
- EGN 5932C Special Topics Engineering
