# Environmental Engineering B.S.Env.E. — Program Overview

## What Do Environmental Engineers Do?
Environmental engineers apply the principles of physics, chemistry, and biology to protect, restore, and sustain human health and critical ecosystems including water, air, and land resources.

## What is the Environmental Engineering Program at FGCU Like?
- Hands-on labs testing water quality, designing biotreatment reactors, and conducting life-cycle assessments
- Small class sizes and accessible faculty
- Undergraduate research in water quality, bioremediation, and hydraulic modeling
- Student organizations like the Florida Water Environment Association (FWEA)
- Industry skills including GIS, ICPR, LCA, and AutoCAD

## Where Do Environmental Engineers Work?
- Water quality and drinking water production
- Air pollution control
- Site restoration
- Stormwater system design
- Sustainability planning
- Wastewater and solid waste management
- Graduate studies including M.S. in Civil Engineering

72% of FGCU environmental engineering graduates stay in Florida.
Average alumni salary: $76,912.

## Accreditation
The B.S.Env.E. program at FGCU is accredited by the Engineering Accreditation Commission of ABET.

## Program Educational Objectives
Graduates are expected to within a few years of graduation:
- Pursue lifelong learning through continuing education and/or advanced degrees
- Progress to professional registration
- Continue professional development through participation in professional organizations

## Student Outcomes
By graduation, students will have:
- Ability to identify, formulate, and solve complex engineering problems
- Ability to apply engineering design with consideration of public health, safety, and welfare
- Ability to communicate effectively with a range of audiences
- Ability to recognize ethical and professional responsibilities
- Ability to function effectively on a team
- Ability to develop and conduct appropriate experimentation and interpret data
- Ability to acquire and apply new knowledge using appropriate learning strategies
