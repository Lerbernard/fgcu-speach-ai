## Combined Program
The combined Bachelors/Master’s program in Computer Science (B.S.) and Computer Science (M.S.) is an accelerated program designed specifically for students who have displayed academic excellence as undergraduate majors. The program provides an opportunity for these students to obtain a bachelor's and master’s degree within a combined period of 5 years. Qualified Students may take up to 9 credits of graduate level courses (5000-6999) during their junior and senior years and have them count towards their bachelor's and master's degrees.
## Combined Program Admission Requirements
  * Completed 75 credits in their B.S. in Computer Science program
  * Major GPA at least 3.4
  * Overall GPA of at least 3.2

Candidates who have completed greater than 90 credits in their B.S. in Computer Science program will need department approval to apply for the combined program. The Candidate must submit their application materials to the graduate program coordinator. The graduate program coordinator is responsible for communicating with the students regarding acceptance of the combined program.
## Combined Program Progression
  * The B.S. in Computer Science program is 120 credits
  * The M.S. in Computer Science program is 30 Credits
  * Up to 9 Credits will be double counted for both the bachelor’s and the master’s programs
  * Students will satisfy all other requirements for the B.S. in Computer Science including general education courses, common prerequisites courses, required courses in the major, restricted electives in the major, and the university’s Sustainability Course Graduation Requirement (SCGR)
  * For a student that has earned 9 graduate credits towards their bachelor’s degree, the total number of credits for the combined program is a minimum of 120 + 21 = 141 credit hours
  * A minimum grade of B- is required for each graduate course to be double-counted

Students will take up to 9 graduate credits from the M.S. in Computer Science program as follows:
  * For students who select the Data Science Concentration: 
    * The student will be able to substitute the 6 credits from the list of 4 classes with CAl 6107 Adv. Machine Learning and CAP 5415 Computer Vision
    * The student will be able to substitute any 3 credit restrictive elective with any 3 credit graduate course required for the M.S. degree
  * For students who select the Cybersecurity Concentration: 
    * The student will be able to substitute CIS 4622 Hands-on Cybersecurity with CAP 5151 Secure Internet of Things
    * The student will be able to substitute CIS 4368 Intro to Database Security with CIS 5377 Info. Security & Compliance
    * The student will be able to substitute any 3 credit restrictive elective with any 3 credit graduate course required for the M.S. degree
  * For students who select the Software Engineering concentration: Only 3 credits will double count 
    * The student will be able to substitute any 3 credit restrictive elective with any 3 credit graduate course required for the M.S. degree
  * For students who select no concentration: 
    * The student will be able to substitute any 9 credits of restrictive elective with any 9 credits of graduate course required for the M.S. degree

Each student is responsible for completing an application to the M.S. in Computer Science program within one semester of graduation from the B.S. in Computer Science program to ensure continuous enrollment under the current catalog. Upon completion of the bachelor's program and acceptance in the graduate program, students will be classified as graduate students. Students will complete an additional 21 graduate credits to satisfy the M.S. in Computer Science requirements as outlined in the program catalog. Up to 9 graduate credits taken while an undergraduate student and counted towards their undergraduate degree will be counted to reach the 30 credits for the M.S in Computer Science as approved by the graduate program coordinator.
Students who begin the combined program as undergraduates but do not continue to the M.S. in Computer Science program may still count any graduate course they took that was accepted to count towards this proposed combined program.
Students that do not successfully complete the bachelor’s degree program will be withdrawn from the M.S. in Computer Science program. These students may reapply to the graduate program upon successfully completing the bachelor's degree and will be required to take the required 30 credits for the M.S. in Computer Science.
