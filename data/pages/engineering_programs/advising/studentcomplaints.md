# Student Concerns and Complaints

At Florida Gulf Coast University (FGCU), student success is at the center of all of our endeavors. There will be occasions where a student encounters a concern or has a matter that requires a resolution. Florida Gulf Coast University is dedicated to treating all members of its community equitably regarding their personal and professional issues. The established procedures aim to deliver students an expedient resolution of their conflicts with Florida Gulf Coast University faculty and/or staff.

In most cases, students are encouraged to initiate the process informally by discussing their concerns directly with the faculty or staff member. Faculty and staff are expected to respond to a student in a reasonable timeframe to assist in the resolution. However, if a student is unable to engage in such a discussion, they may consider contacting the direct supervisor for the faculty, staff, or department.

Academic Colleges and other university offices and responsible units may establish their own policies and procedures for addressing specific types of concerns and complaints. If a student is not able to resolve a matter, the student may file a written complaint or use a specific appeals process (if one applies to their concern/complaint). Additional information regarding student concerns and complaints can be found in FGCU Policy 4.017.

## What is a Concern or Complaint?
Students may express concerns or complaints regarding various academic-related issues or non-academic-related issues. Issues and concerns may include but are not limited to: mistreatment by university faculty and staff, erroneous fee calculations, errors in registration and record-keeping, student employment concerns, classroom and grade-related grievances, among others.

Allegations such as discrimination, harassment, and/or sexual harassment based on race, color, ethnicity, religion, sex, age, marital status, national origin, genetic information, or disability may also be included in complaints. To submit a formal complaint a student may contact the Office of Institutional Ethics and Compliance (OIEC) or report through EthicsPoint Hotline.

Throughout the entirety of the process, students have the option of reaching out to the Ombuds Office or the Dean of Students to assist with an informal resolution to any concerns or complaints. The Ombuds Office is available to listen to your concerns, clarify policies and procedures, help identify various options, and work with you to find a resolution to the matter. Similarly, the Dean of Students Office can serve as an advocate or resource in many situations.

We strongly encourage students to address any concerns by initiating a dialogue with the relevant faculty, staff, or administrators, or with their supervisors, as necessary. If such an informal discussion proves unfeasible or fails to produce a satisfactory outcome, the student may escalate the matter to the next level of authority. This may involve contacting the associate dean, college dean, director, or other appropriate administrators or designee in an attempt to resolve the issue informally. In the event that an informal resolution is not possible, and/or the student wishes to pursue a formal process, they may proceed accordingly.

## University Formal Complaint Options

**Academic Complaint Form (Non-Grade Related)** — Responsible Unit: Academic Affairs. This form is only used for instances not already covered in a formal process, in accordance with the Student Complaint Policy. A non-grade related written student complaint is in the academic setting or classroom that is not grade related. This may include an allegation of improper, unfair, or arbitrary action by an FGCU department, administrator, and/or faculty member.

**Non-Academic Complaint Form (Outside the Classroom)** — Responsible Unit: Dean of Students. This form is only used for instances not already covered in a formal process, in accordance with the Student Complaint Policy. A non-academic written student complaint is an allegation of improper, unfair, or arbitrary action by an FGCU department, administrator, and/or staff member involving the application of a specific provision of an FGCU regulation, policy, or procedure.

**Office of Institutional Ethics and Compliance** — Responsible Unit: Office of Institutional Ethics and Compliance. Filing: FGCU EthicsPoint Hotline. Policy/Regulation: FGCU Policy 1.006.

## Academic Appeals

**Academic Integrity / Misconduct Appeal** — Responsible Unit: Office of Student Conduct. Filing: Student Conduct Appeal Form. Policy/Regulation: FGCU Regulation 4.002.

**Admission Decision Appeal** — Responsible Unit: Admissions & Financial Aid. Undergraduates who wish to appeal must do so in writing by email to undergrad@fgcu.edu. Graduate students who wish to appeal must do so in writing to the graduate program representative or college designee. Policy/Regulation: FGCU Regulation 2.001 (UG) and FGCU Regulation 3.005 (Grad).

**Grade Appeal** — Responsible Unit: Academic Affairs. Filing: Student Grade Appeals Details.

**Satisfactory Academic Progress Appeal** — Responsible Unit: Financial Aid & Scholarship. Students must log into Gulfline to access the Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP) Appeal form.

## Non-Academic Appeals

**Appeal of Disability Accommodation Request** — Responsible Unit: Office of Institutional Ethics and Compliance. Filing: FGCU EthicsPoint Hotline. Policy/Regulation: FGCU Policy 1.008.

**FAFSA Change in Circumstance Appeal** — Responsible Unit: Financial Aid & Scholarships. Policy/Regulation: Change In Circumstances Website.

**Fraternity & Sorority Life Expansion Appeal** — Responsible Unit: Fraternity & Sorority Life. An organization may appeal the expansion decision within three (3) class days from the date of the decision. Contact fsl@fgcu.edu. Policy/Regulation: Expansion Bylaws.

**Housing Agreement Cancellation Appeal** — Responsible Unit: Housing & Residence Life. If a student's cancellation request is denied, the student receives an email outlining the housing appeals process. Policy/Regulation: Housing Agreements.

**Housing Fee Appeal** — Responsible Unit: Housing & Residence Life. If a student receives a damage fee or lockout/lock change fee, the student receives an email communication outlining the charge appeals process. Housing cancellation fees are not eligible for appeal. Policy/Regulation: Housing Agreements.

**Parking Services Citation Appeal** — Responsible Unit: Parking Services. Login to the FGCU Parking Portal account to file an appeal. Policy/Regulation: FGCU Regulation 8.006.

**Residency Reclassification Appeal** — Responsible Unit: Enrollment Services Center. Filing: Residency Reclassification Form. Policy/Regulation: Residency Reclassification Procedures.

**Student and Student Organization Conduct Appeal** — Responsible Unit: Office of Student Conduct. Filing: Student Conduct Appeal Form. Policy/Regulation: FGCU Regulation 4.002.

**Student Activities Eligibility Appeal** — Responsible Unit: Office of Student Involvement. Appeals must be submitted within 5 days of receiving the notification. Contact osi@fgcu.edu. Policy/Regulation: FGCU Policy 4.008.

**Tuition and Fee Appeal** — Responsible Unit: Department of Records & Registration. Filing: Fee Adjustment Request Form. Policy/Regulation: FGCU Regulation 7.001.

**Withdrawal with Extenuating Circumstances Appeal** — Responsible Unit: Dean of Students. Filing: Withdrawal with Extenuating Circumstances Form. Policy/Regulation: Withdrawal with Extenuating Circumstances Appeal Criteria.

## Other Complaints

**Alleged Discrimination Violations** — Responsible Unit: Office of Institutional Ethics and Compliance. Filing: FGCU EthicsPoint Hotline. Policy/Regulation: FGCU Policy 1.006.

**Alleged FERPA Violation** — Responsible Unit: Department of Records & Registration. FERPA allows students to file a complaint about the university's failure to comply with FERPA requirements at the Family Policy Compliance Office. Policy/Regulation: FGCU Policy 3.006.

**Alleged Title IX Violations** — Responsible Unit: Office of Institutional Ethics and Compliance. Filing: FGCU EthicsPoint Hotline. Policy/Regulation: FGCU Policy 1.006.

**FL-SARA Complaints** — Responsible Unit: Postsecondary Reciprocal Distance Education Coordinating Council. Distance education students, who have completed the internal FGCU grievance process and the applicable state process as outlined by the Florida Board of Governors, may appeal non-instructional complaints to the FL-SARA PRDEC Council.

**Student Health Services** — Responsible Unit: Student Health Services. Filing: Privacy Practices. Policy/Regulation: Notice of Privacy Practices-Complaints.

**SACSCOC Complaints** — Responsible Unit: Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges. Filing: File a Complaint. Policy/Regulation: SACSCOC Policy.

**SUS BOG Complaints** — Responsible Unit: State of Florida Board of Governors. Filing: File a Complaint. Policy/Regulation: BOG Assistance for Solving Problems.
