# Construction Management B.S.C.M. — Program Overview

## What Do Construction Managers Do?
Construction managers manage, coordinate, and supervise construction projects from start to finish, including scheduling, budgeting, and managing materials and people.

## What is the Construction Management Program at FGCU Like?
- Strong balance between theoretical knowledge and real-world experience
- Faculty includes both full-time academics and active industry professionals
- Undergraduate research in building information modeling (BIM) and AI in construction
- Student organizations including Associated Builders and Contractors (ABC) and Women in Construction (WIC)
- Earn your 30-hour OSHA card during the program
- Construction management courses throughout all four years, not just junior and senior years

## Industry Connections
- Senior capstone projects provided and mentored by industry partners
- Nearly 100% of courses incorporate guest lectures from industry experts
- Industry-led demonstrations (framing, drywall, masonry) incorporated into classes
- Curriculum reviewed by the Construction Industry Advisory Board (CIAB)

## Where Do Construction Managers Work?
- Project Engineer
- Assistant Project Manager
- Assistant Superintendent

81% of FGCU construction management graduates stay in Florida.
Average alumni salary: $72,293. Approximately 85% of students receive job offers before graduation.

## Accreditation
The B.S.C.M. program at FGCU is accredited by the American Council for Construction Education (ACCE).

## Program Goals
- Support students in timely and successfully joining the construction industry workforce
- Provide an educational environment in which students and faculty can excel
- Engage constituents to foster the impact of the Construction Management program in Southwest Florida
- Reinforce program capacity to better serve students and faculty
