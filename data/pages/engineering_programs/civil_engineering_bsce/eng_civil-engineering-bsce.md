# Civil Engineering B.S.C.E. — Program Overview

## What Do Civil Engineers Do?
Civil engineers focus on the design, construction, and maintenance of large public and private infrastructure projects such as buildings, bridges, roads, and water systems.

## What is the Civil Engineering Program at FGCU Like?
- Hands-on coursework testing concrete, designing CAD projects, geotechnology labs, traffic simulations, water modeling, and field surveying
- Small class sizes and accessible faculty
- Undergraduate research in hydrogeology, geotechnology, climate resiliency, and water resources
- Student organizations like the American Society of Civil Engineers (ASCE)
- Industry standard skills including GIS and AutoCAD

## Where Do Civil Engineers Work?
- Land Development
- Project Management
- Graduate studies including M.S. in Civil Engineering

76% of FGCU civil engineering graduates stay in Florida.
Average alumni salary: $79,217.

## Accreditation
The B.S.C.E. Civil Engineering program at FGCU is accredited by the Engineering Accreditation Commission of ABET.

## Program Educational Objectives
Graduates are expected to within a few years of graduation:
- Pursue lifelong learning through continuing education and/or advanced degrees
- Progress to professional registration
- Continue professional development through participation in professional organizations

## Student Outcomes
By graduation, students will have:
- Ability to identify, formulate, and solve complex engineering problems
- Ability to apply engineering design with consideration of public health, safety, and welfare
- Ability to communicate effectively with a range of audiences
- Ability to recognize ethical and professional responsibilities
- Ability to function effectively on a team
- Ability to develop and conduct appropriate experimentation and interpret data
- Ability to acquire and apply new knowledge using appropriate learning strategies
