# Civil Engineering B.S.C.E. — Degree Map

## Program Overview
The Bachelor of Science in Civil Engineering at FGCU is an ABET-accredited program focused on the conception, design, construction, and maintenance of large public and private infrastructure projects. The program requires 122 total credits.

Civil engineers are responsible for creating sustainable infrastructure in areas including construction, geotechnical, structural, transportation, and water resources.

Average salary for FGCU civil engineering alumni: $79,217.
76% of graduates stay in Florida.

## Graduation Requirements
- Complete a minimum of 122 credits
- At least 42 upper division credits (3000-4999 level)
- Cumulative GPA of 2.0 at FGCU
- Minimum grade of C in each required course
- Satisfy Service-Learning, Civic Literacy, and Sustainability requirements
- Complete 30 of the last 60 credits at FGCU including 12 in the major
- CGN 4802 and ENV 4612C must be taken at FGCU
- ENV 4612C satisfies the Sustainability Course Graduation Requirement

## Combined B.S.C.E. / M.S. Program

Qualified students can complete both the B.S.C.E. and M.S. in Civil Engineering in approximately 5 years.

**Admission Requirements:**
- Junior standing or higher
- Major GPA of at least 3.5
- Overall GPA of at least 3.2

**Program Details:**
- B.S.C.E. is 122 credits, M.S. is 30 credits
- Up to 9 graduate credits double-counted for both degrees
- Total combined program: 145 credit hours
- Minimum grade of B- required for each double-counted graduate course
- Students remain classified as Undergraduates until bachelor's degree is awarded

**Approved graduate courses for double-counting include:**
- BCN 6705 Construction Management
- BCN 6748 Construction Law
- CWR 6537 Contaminant Transport
- CWR 6637 Storm Water Management and Modeling
- EGN 6430 Advanced Engineering Analysis
- EGN 6457 Research Methods
- ENV 6516 Sustainable Water Quality Engineering
- CGN 5320 GIS in Civil and Environmental
- CGN 5716 Environmental Data Science
