# Bioengineering B.S. — Degree Map

## Program Overview
The Bachelor of Science in Bioengineering at FGCU combines engineering principles with biology and physiology to solve problems regarding human health and quality of life. The program is ABET-accredited and requires 125 total credits.

Graduates are prepared for careers in:
- Medical Device Development
- Quality Assurance
- Medical school and graduate studies

Average starting salary for FGCU bioengineering alumni: $72,984.
66% of graduates stay in Florida.

## Program Educational Objectives
Graduates are expected to attain within a few years of graduation:
- Technical competence as bioengineers and recognition as contributors in their communities
- Accomplishment in communicating and working collaboratively in a diverse, multidisciplinary environment
- Proficiency in using entrepreneurial and learning skills to adapt to a global society

## Student Outcomes
By graduation, students will have:
- Ability to identify, formulate, and solve complex engineering problems
- Ability to apply engineering design with consideration of public health, safety, and welfare
- Ability to communicate effectively with a range of audiences
- Ability to recognize ethical and professional responsibilities
- Ability to function effectively on a team
- Ability to develop and conduct appropriate experimentation and interpret data
- Ability to acquire and apply new knowledge using appropriate learning strategies
