# Student Care Services

Student Care Services supports the person behind the student, talking through concerns and coordinating with campus partners to connect students with resources as they navigate personal challenges, distress, and barriers to well-being.

## Care & Concern Referral
If you believe a student could benefit from outreach by Student Care Services, fill out a Care & Concern Referral Form.

## Support Offered
- Essentials Closet: Free access to essential items such as toiletries and basic school supplies.
- Withdrawal for Extenuating Circumstances: Supports students facing unforeseen events beyond their control that prevent them from completing current or previous semesters.
- Class Absence Notice: Notifies instructors when a student experiences an emergency or unexpected situation impacting attendance for five or more consecutive class days.
- Basic Needs Initiatives: Assists students experiencing homelessness, housing insecurity, or who have experienced foster care.
- QPR Suicide Prevention: Free certification equipping students, faculty, and staff with skills to recognize warning signs of suicide, intervene, and connect individuals to support resources.

## When to Make a Referral
You should especially consider referring a student who is:
- Displaying sudden changes in behavior or attendance
- Going through personal or family emergencies
- Showing signs of emotional distress
- Facing financial struggles or housing or food insecurity
- In any challenging situation where you are unsure how to help

Students are encouraged to contact Student Care Services directly at 239-590-7900.

## Who We Are
Student Care Services assists students in managing life stressors that may impact academic success and personal well-being, offering guidance, resources, and referrals. When a student requests assistance or the office receives a referral, staff make every effort to connect with the student through interventions, university services, and community referrals, and assist students seeking to withdraw due to extenuating circumstances. The priority is to support student well-being and safety by intervening early, before concerns escalate to a crisis. For urgent mental health support, contact CAPS at 239-590-7950, or outside business hours the CAPS helpline at 239-745-3277. For urgent help related to sexual violence or sexual harassment, contact FGCU's confidential Victim Advocate, Aisha Lewis, at 239-745-4344.

## What We Do
To support students, Student Care Services may reach out via email or phone to offer an appointment, assist students returning to campus after hospitalization, provide ongoing support and follow-up for high-risk students, connect students with campus and community resources, encourage self-advocacy skills, and advocate for students at individual and systemic levels. The office coordinates care across campus partners including CAPS, Adaptive Services, Student Health Services, Academic Advising, Financial Aid & Scholarships, and University Recreation & Wellness.

## What to Expect
If you receive a letter from Student Care Services, it means information suggests you may benefit from a supportive conversation and connection to resources. Student Care Services is not part of Student Conduct or the Office of Institutional Ethics and Compliance; outreach is non-punitive and intended solely to support well-being. An initial meeting typically lasts about 30 minutes, discussing your situation and available options across areas such as mental and emotional health, academics, living environment, or financial concerns. Follow-up or check-in appointments (often 30-minute, in-person, or by phone, email, or video) continue discussing progress, resources, and well-being. Post-Hospital Services help students transition back to campus after a behavioral health hospitalization. Class Absence Notifications: students should notify instructors as soon as possible; after appropriate documentation, Student Care Services can provide a courtesy notification to faculty outlining the general situation and anticipated duration, which does not automatically excuse absences or guarantee accommodations.

## Who Can Refer
Anyone can submit a referral. Common referral sources include faculty and staff, parents and family members, law enforcement, and medical and care facilities (with student permission). Federal law requires protecting student privacy; in most cases the office cannot share specific information about a student without their consent. Information shared is protected under educational privacy laws (FERPA), which allows university faculty and staff to share relevant observations or concerns with officials who have a legitimate educational interest, and permits sharing with parents, law enforcement, or others when necessary to protect health or safety.

## How to Make a Referral
Complete a report and include as much detail as possible in the "Description/Narrative" section — an objective account of observed behaviors, concerns, statements, or written communication, with specific examples, direct quotes, and relevant documentation. If you have a relationship with the student, it is often helpful to let them know you plan to submit a referral and why. Some referrals may be reviewed in collaboration with campus teams such as the Behavioral Consultation and Assessment Team (BCAT) and Threat Assessment & Management Team (TAM).
