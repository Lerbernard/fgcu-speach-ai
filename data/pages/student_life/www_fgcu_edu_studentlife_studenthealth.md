# Student Health Services

## Class Absence Request Form
Unforeseen events may cause a student to be absent from class, including illness, medical issues, bereavement, accident, hospitalization, pregnancy and/or parenting, or a catastrophic event. Use the class absence notice request form from Student Care Services. Class absences are generally sent when the student is out for five days or longer, or when their condition does not allow them to communicate directly with each professor in a timely manner. This form is not reviewed after-hours, on weekends, or during university holidays or breaks. To report an emergency, call the University Police Department at (239) 590-1900, or dial 911.

## Clinic Hours
Monday–Thursday 8:15am–4:45pm, Friday 9:00am–4:45pm. There is also an after-hours nursing line.

## Insurance & Billing
FGCU does not offer a domestic insurance plan. There is an international student plan coordinated by the Global Engagement Office. All visits to Student Health Services, regardless of insurance coverage, are free. Some medications, labs and supplies are provided at nominal prices. Students should bring some form of payment to each appointment to cover the possibility of a charge.

## Routine Care
Routine medical care and preventive medicine, women's healthcare, health screenings, contraceptive counseling, referrals for specialist care, uncomplicated wound care, health education, flu shots, laboratory testing through Quest Lab, rapid in-house tests for strep/mono/flu/COVID-19, pregnancy testing, emergency contraceptive, minor surgical procedures, pulmonary function testing, immunizations (measles, mumps, rubella, hepatitis A&B, tetanus), TB screening, Pap smears, confidential HIV testing, STI testing and treatment, dispensing of commonly prescribed medications, physical exams, IV fluids for illness, wart care, nebulizer treatments, X-rays and diagnostic testing, specialty physician care, overnight infirmary services, allergy injections, chronic disease management, drug testing, physical therapy, mental-health care (prescribing of ADD/ADHD medications), and a travel clinic for overseas immunization requirements.

## Privacy (HIPAA)
Student Health Services (SHS) operates in strict adherence to HIPAA laws and regulations to protect your protected health information (PHI). SHS creates a record of the services you receive to provide quality care and comply with legal requirements, and will not disclose more of your PHI than necessary. The Notice of Privacy Practices includes detailed information on how patients can obtain a copy of their health information, request amendments, receive an accounting of disclosures, request restrictions, and more.

## Free STI Testing
With CAN Community Health, on-campus FREE STI testing is offered 2–4 times each month, usually on Thursdays from 10 a.m.–2 p.m.

## UBYou Wellness App
UBYou is the Student Wellness Success Hub designed for students by students, and is FREE for FGCU students (sign up with your FGCU student email). The app offers a daily wellness check-in to reflect on your day, physical and mental health exercises, how-to videos, breathing exercises, meditations, and workout demonstrations, and connects you to a hub of campus resources. Download from your device's app store and complete your profile with your university credentials.
