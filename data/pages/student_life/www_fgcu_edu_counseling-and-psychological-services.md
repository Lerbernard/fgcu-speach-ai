# Counseling and Psychological Services (CAPS)

Counseling and Psychological Services (CAPS) is the main source of mental health support and therapy services on campus.

## Make an Appointment
To schedule, call CAPS at 239-590-7950 or visit the office in person. Appointments last about an hour. Email: caps@fgcu.edu
Before you arrive:
- Bring your Eagle ID to all appointments.
- Missed appointments not canceled at least 24 hours ahead are subject to a no-show fee.
- Telehealth and in-person appointments are offered.
- Share a few details so staff understand your needs and guide you to the right support.

CAPS staff meet with you, learn what you need, explain your options, match you with a provider, schedule your follow-up visit, and direct you to community services if you need more support.

## Virtual Front Desk
Send an encrypted message for general inquiries or scheduling needs. Expect a response the same business day. The CAPS Virtual Front Desk is open 9 a.m. to 4 p.m. and messages are only monitored during those hours. If this is a crisis or emergency, contact 911 or the CAPS Crisis Hotline at 239-745-3277. Email: caps@fgcu.edu

## Hours and Location
CAPS is open 8:00am–5:00pm, closed for lunch 12:00pm–12:30pm, Monday–Friday. CAPS is located on the 3rd floor of the Student and Community Counseling Center. The nearest parking is in garage 2 or 4; the lot next to the building is reserved for staff/faculty and Community Counseling Center clients, with no student parking.

## In Crisis?
If you feel like you may be in danger of hurting yourself or others, call 911 or go to the nearest hospital. FGCU students in immediate distress outside of normal business hours (evenings, weekends, and holidays) who want to speak with a mental health professional can call the CAPS crisis line at 239-745-3277. Email: caps@fgcu.edu

## Eligibility
The center is fully accredited by the International Accreditation of Counseling Services, Inc. Most services are covered for students enrolled in the current semester and/or who have paid health fees for the current academic term. CAPS cannot provide services for students not enrolled in the current semester, alumni, faculty, or staff. One exception: students taking one semester off between two enrolled semesters can pay a Health Bridge fee ($54) to bridge the gap in services. The fee can be paid electronically after visiting the CAPS office.

## Services Provided
Individual Counseling, Relationship Counseling, Group Counseling, Psychiatric Services, Case Management, Consultation, Outreach Programs, Crisis Intervention, Testing for LD and/or ADHD, Alcohol and/or Marijuana Use Psychoeducational Group, Suicide Prevention Programs, Nutritional Counseling, Referrals to Community Resources, Relationship Self-Assessments, and Online Presentations and Self-Help Resources. Formalized LD/ADHD testing is offered at a reduced rate. The psychoeducational group (typically for sanctioned students) incurs a cost.

## Attendance Policy
Due to high demand, this policy applies to students receiving any clinical service:
- You can opt in to a courtesy text reminder system. Opting in or out does not change any no-show charge.
- Clients who do not show are charged a no-show fee: $25 for any clinician, appointment type, or length (not including group sessions), and $45 for the psychiatrist or LD/ADHD testing. It appears on your FGCU account as a miscellaneous medical charge. You are charged a no-show or late cancellation fee if you do not cancel at least 24 hours before your appointment.
- Clients should cancel or change appointments as far in advance as possible. You may leave a message on nights/weekends (24-hour notice required). Do not use the HelpLine to change or cancel an appointment.
- For recurring appointments, a cancel or no-show is assumed to end the recurring series unless you ask to be rescheduled.
- Clients who show a pattern of no-shows or cancellations may have eligibility for services suspended. Clinicians reserve the right to suspend or terminate services after three missed (canceled or no-show) appointments.

## Other Resources
Togetherall is an online peer-to-peer platform offering a safe space to connect with others experiencing similar feelings. CAPS also offers anonymous mental health screenings for depression, anxiety, substance abuse, or other issues from any computer or smart device. Faculty and staff can use the FGCU Community Counseling Center or the Employee Assistance Program (CAPS cannot offer therapy to faculty or staff).
