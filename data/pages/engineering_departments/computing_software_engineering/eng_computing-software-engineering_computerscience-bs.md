# Computer Science B.S. — Program Overview

## What Do Computer Science Students Learn?
Computer science students build a strong foundation in theory and algorithms while taking specialized courses in cybersecurity, natural language processing, and data science.

## What is the Computer Science Program at FGCU Like?
- Hands-on experience with AI, computer and network design, and data modeling
- Small class sizes and accessible faculty
- Undergraduate research in AI, cybersecurity, and data science
- Student organizations like the Computer Science and Software Engineering Club (CSSEC)
- Specialized courses in human computer interaction, robotics, NLP, digital systems, neural networks, data mining, machine learning, and assembly language

## Concentrations
- Cybersecurity — database security, network security, hands-on cybersecurity labs
- AI and Data Science — natural language processing, neural networks, deep learning, exploratory data analysis, data mining
- Software Engineering — requirements engineering, software testing, software architecture and design

## Where Do Computer Science Majors Work?
- Software Developers
- Project Managers
- Business Analysts
- System and Network Engineers
- Data Scientists
- Cybersecurity and AI roles

66% of FGCU computer science graduates stay in Florida.
Average alumni salary: $88,542.

## Accreditation
The B.S. Computer Science program is accredited by ABET.

## Program Educational Objectives
Graduates are expected to within a few years of graduation:
- Leverage computer science theories and principles to contribute meaningfully in professional roles or graduate studies
- Practice life-long learning, adapting to the rapidly changing technological world

## Student Outcomes
By graduation, graduates will have ability to:
- Analyze complex computing problems and apply principles to identify solutions
- Design, implement, and evaluate a computing-based solution
- Communicate effectively in professional contexts
- Recognize professional responsibilities and make informed ethical judgments
- Function effectively as a member or leader of a team
- Apply computer science theory and software development fundamentals to produce solutions
