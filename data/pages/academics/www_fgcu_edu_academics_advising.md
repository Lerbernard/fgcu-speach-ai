# University Advising Services (UAS)

University Advising Services provides advising for all undergraduate students enrolled at Florida Gulf Coast University.

## What is Academic Advising?
Academic Advising at FGCU begins at Orientation. As students progress, professional advisors match individual needs with available resources in the field of study with the goal of keeping students on track to graduate.

## What do Academic Advisors do?
Academic advisors guide students in their transition into and navigation of the university experience. Our staff helps students clarify their interests, skills and values in the context of class selection, major choice, and curriculum planning. We empower students to make informed academic decisions while helping them interpret policy and understand the university resources available to assist in their academic success.

## We Provide Advising Services Through:
- Advising Appointments: Students may schedule an advising appointment online at their advising area website or by calling their advising area main phone line. Scheduled appointments are best for assistance that requires significant time for discussion and planning (typically 30–40 minutes).
- Drop-in Advising: Available during the first week of classes and throughout the semester for quick registration questions or issues (typically 10–15 minutes). Visit your advising area webpage for specific drop-in days and times.
- Email: Students may email their advising area for assistance with general advising questions or concerns.
- Phone: Students may call the main phone line of their advising area for general questions or to schedule an appointment.
- In-person assistance: Students may come in person to their advising area office for general questions or to schedule an appointment.

## Find Your Academic Advisor
Your advisor is determined by a combination of your current class status and declared major. Students can find their academic advisor listed on the top of their Degree Works audit in Gulfline.

Most freshmen students are advised by Exploratory Advising. Students are eligible to transition to college advising after they meet certain academic requirements. If you are a transfer student or have already transitioned, you are advised by the college advising office that houses your major: College of Arts & Sciences, Lutgert College of Business, College of Education, Marieb College of Health & Human Services, U.A. Whitaker College of Engineering, or School of Entrepreneurship.

## Find Your Advising Office

**First Time In College**
All first-year students are advised by the Exploratory Advising Office, which guides new students in their transition into and navigation of the university experience.
- Location: The Center for Career & Exploratory Advising
- Office Hours: Monday–Friday, 8:00am–5:00pm
- Phone: 239-745-4422
- Email: explore@fgcu.edu
Drop-in advising is available during the first week of classes for each semester to assist with registration issues. First-year students will transition to college advising (based on their declared major/academic program) once they meet the transition criteria for their program.

**Sophomores, Juniors or Seniors**
All students who have transitioned to college advising will be advised by the advising office in the college that houses their major. For the U.A. Whitaker College of Engineering:
- Location: Holmes Hall 207
- Office Hours: Monday–Friday, 8:00am–5:00pm
- Phone: 239-590-1445
- Email: WCEAdvising@fgcu.edu

**Undeclared and Exploratory Students**
Students who are exploring or Undeclared beyond their freshman year transition date are advised by Exploratory Advising. Walk-in advising is only available during the first week of classes for each semester to assist with registration issues.

**Dual Enrollment Programs (ACE & Collegiate Academy)**
Exploratory Advising serves students in this program while dual-enrolled and up until high school graduation. After high school graduation, students continuing their undergraduate degrees at FGCU will transition to college advising.

**Student-Athletes**
Student-athletes should first check in with the Hartley Academic Resource Center (HARC).

**Academic Hold Students**
Students with an Academic Hold for an FGCU GPA of <2.0 are required to meet with their academic advisor. Please review the Academic Standing Policy.

**Recently Admitted Transfer Students**
Recently admitted transfer students will meet their academic advisors at orientation. While at FGCU, they are advised by the advising office in the college that houses their major. If undeclared, they are advised by Exploratory Advising. For the U.A. Whitaker College of Engineering:
- Location: Holmes Hall 207
- Office Hours: Monday–Friday, 8:00am–5:00pm
- Phone: 239-590-1445
- Email: WCEAdvising@fgcu.edu

**Prospective Students**
Prospective students first need to contact the Office of Undergraduate Admissions.

**Returning Students**
Please visit Undergraduate Admissions for details.

**Non-Degree Seeking Students**
Non-degree-seeking students do not have an assigned advisor. They are encouraged to contact the department in which the courses they wish to take are housed.
