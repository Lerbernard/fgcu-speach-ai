# Global Engagement Office (GEO)

The Global Engagement Team promotes global learning as an integral part of the education provided by FGCU that enriches the student, the Southwest Florida community, and the world to which it connects.

Areas include: Global Outreach and Engagement, Study Abroad & International Education, International Students, Intensive English Language Program, International Travel, COIL/VE, International Scholars, and International Agreements.

## Apply for a New Passport at FGCU
The Global Engagement Office serves as a Passport Application Acceptance Facility for the FGCU community and the general public. The office is authorized by the U.S. Department of State to execute and mail-in Form DS-11 passport applications. Schedule an appointment to apply. Phone: (239) 590-1485.

## FGCU Fulbright Scholar Top Off Award Program
To encourage the pursuit of Fulbright Scholars, FGCU maintains the current base salary for all full-time faculty members who win a Fulbright US Scholar Program Award for the duration of their award.

## FGCU Global Virtual Content
An innovative way for FGCU faculty, students and staff to explore the world and internationalize experience, whether built into the curriculum or for virtual educational adventure. Anyone with an FGCU staff or eagle email address has access to discoverED 360, a learning experience platform offering educational content in Humanities, Sciences, Business, and Global Perspectives.

## Programs and Opportunities

**GEO Ambassador Program**
Provides opportunities for international students to experience American culture through friendships with U.S. students, and helps American students expand their education through interaction with students from abroad.

**Passport Acceptance Facility**
FGCU is an authorized Passport Acceptance Facility serving students, faculty, staff and the public.

**Peace Corps Prep Program**
A certificate program for undergraduates that builds competencies by integrating coursework with hands-on experience and professional development, centered on preparing to be the best Peace Corps Volunteer (or international fieldwork representative) possible.

**Study Abroad**
By immersing yourself in another culture, you broaden your international perspective and gain greater awareness of your own culture and history. International experience leads to greater employment opportunities after graduation.

**Exchange Programs**
Exchange programs allow you to pay FGCU tuition to study at foreign institutions with which FGCU has exchange agreements. Students earn direct FGCU academic credit for courses taken abroad. Exchange programs are often affiliated with and coordinated by a college or degree program.

**Global Learning Grant Program**
Helps undergraduate students pay for tuition and fees in programs affiliated with the Global Engagement Office, provided they meet eligibility requirements. First-year students and/or students with a GPA of 3.5 or higher should apply for the LAC scholarship through FGCU Admissions.

**Fulbright Program**
Established in 1946, the Fulbright Program aims to increase mutual understanding between the peoples of the United States and other countries through the exchange of persons, knowledge, and skills. FGCU Faculty Advisor: Michael McDonald (PhD).

**Collaborative Online International Learning (COIL)**
A teaching method that enables professors from different countries to add a global learning experience to their courses.

## International Travel

**International Travel Registry: ABEONA**
When traveling to international destinations, faculty, staff and students must seek approval by registering their travel in FGCU's Abeona Travel Registry system. FGCU faculty and staff must seek approval from their individual units and departments when traveling for official FGCU business. A travel authorization request should be entered as soon as the travel is known but no later than 45 days before the planned departure per the FGCU International Travel Policy. For questions related to the FGCU International Travel Policy, contact Dr. Mike McDonald at (239) 590-7925. For questions about the Abeona process or specific approved travel, email abeona@fgcu.edu.

**Travel Approval Timing**
Allow a minimum of 2 weeks for approval to be processed once your supervisor has approved your request; this may be longer depending on the information needed and destination.

**Who is required to register travel?**
All FGCU faculty, staff and some students traveling abroad for official "Mission Critical" University-related business. Mission Critical Travel includes travel to participate in or conduct conferences, workshops, conventions or professional association meetings; to conduct scholarly activities including performances, exhibitions, and research; to conduct service activities; and to conduct grant-related activities as allowed by the grant. Travel should be registered at least 45 days prior to departure, and registrations updated as information changes.

**Faculty or Staff Traveling With Students**
If traveling with 4 or fewer students, have the students complete their own travel request in the system. If traveling with a group of more than 4 students, the faculty/staff member must first ensure all students have created a traveler profile in Abeona, then complete the Request for International Travel Approval and select student names from the registry. All travelers create a Spend Authorization in Workday after receiving email approval, register in the Smart Traveler Enrollment Program (STEP), and enroll in CISI Insurance for International Travel.

**International Travel Policy**
International Travel means travel to destinations outside of the fifty United States. U.S. territories such as American Samoa, Guam, Republic of the Marshall Islands, Puerto Rico and the U.S. Virgin Islands are included in this definition. FGCU Policy 2.020 was approved by President Martin on November 13, 2020.

**Travel Insurance**
All FGCU faculty, staff and students traveling internationally on University Sponsored International Travel are required to be covered by the CISI Travel Abroad Insurance Plan. Faculty and staff enrollment forms are provided upon travel destination approval. Students traveling with an official FGCU Faculty Led Program are enrolled in the CISI coverage plan automatically.
