# Service-Learning — Student Resources

Service-Learning is unpaid time spent serving the needs of a nonprofit or government agency while enhancing classroom learning, gaining career-readiness skills, exploring passions, and engaging in community.

## Make Your Service-Learning Count

**Step 1: Explore Service-Learning Opportunities**
Search Eagle Link using key words to find relevant opportunities. Search by your major/minor (e.g., Psychology, Engineering, Finance), by NACE Career Readiness Competencies (skills you want to develop, like Technology, Teamwork, Professionalism), by "Ongoing" (opportunities that occur more than once), by "Virtual" (experiences you can complete online), or by "Guided" (preplanned experiences with transportation provided — just RSVP and show up). Try to find opportunities that connect to your major or future career goals. When reaching out to a community partner by email or phone, include who you are and what you're passionate about, why you're reaching out (a class or graduation requirement), and your contact information and availability. When in doubt, create an Eagle Service Network form and await review.

**Step 2: Create a Service-Learning Form in Eagle Service Network**
Submit and monitor your Eagle Service Network form. This ensures your service experience is reviewed, approved, and officially qualifies for service-learning hours. Check the status until it is approved or rejected:
- Pending: The form is awaiting review by the office.
- Approved: The form has been reviewed by the office and the community partner and meets service-learning qualifications. Now you can serve.
- Rejected: The form does not meet qualifications. Review the comment by clicking edit on the ESN form, then resubmit if applicable.

**Step 3: Track Your Service-Learning Hours and Reflect**
These are required steps for hours to be applied toward your graduation requirement.
Track: Click the track hours button under the title of your service experience and scroll down to the green TRACK HOURS button (do not use the dropdown bar). Wait until you have concluded the experience or the semester is over to track the total number of hours — once you track hours on a form, you cannot add more. The community partner final evaluation email is sent to your supervisor to verify your hours; allow a business day for them to receive it. The supervisor must select YES or NO to verify or deny the hours.
Reflect: Complete the Self Evaluation after your experience. This is required for your hours to count. Click the Self Evaluation button under your ESN form, answer the questions, and submit.
Checking status: If your supervisor verifies your hours and you have completed the self evaluation, the hours transfer to Degree Works. If the hours are not on Degree Works after a few business days but you believe you completed all steps, review the FAQ.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Why aren't my Service-Learning hours showing in Degree Works, and how do I know how many hours I have verified?**
Once hours are tracked and verified, you can view your total verified Service-Learning hours on the top banner of Degree Works. The status dropdown on your form is not a reliable indicator of whether hours will appear. If hours are not appearing, confirm all of the following: the form was submitted through Eagle Service Network; the form was approved by the office (approved does not mean hours are tracked or verified); hours were tracked using the green "Add Hours" button (hours can only be entered once per form); the student Self Evaluation was submitted; and the Community Partner Evaluation was completed (your supervisor responded YES or NO).

**How many Service-Learning hours do I have to complete, and when are they due?**
Most undergraduate students at FGCU are required to complete 80 Service-Learning hours. Upper-level transfer students are typically required to complete 40 hours. Because requirements can vary, always confirm your specific requirement in Degree Works. The deadline is listed on the Academic Calendar for the semester you plan to graduate. Students are strongly encouraged to complete hours as early as possible to avoid graduation delays.

**Do my Service-Learning experiences have to relate to my major or be with an agency in the Community Partner Database?**
No. Experiences do not need to be related to your major or minor, though choosing experiences that support your career goals and résumé is strongly recommended. You are also not required to serve with agencies in the Community Partner Database — students may serve with any nonprofit or government agency, regardless of location, as long as the experience meets Service-Learning guidelines and is approved through the Eagle Service Network.

**Why hasn't my supervisor verified my Service-Learning hours?**
Supervisors must complete the Community Partner Evaluation sent by Symplicity to verify your hours. Click the Evaluation under your form to view their response. If your hours have not been verified, confirm you have tracked your total hours and follow up with your supervisor to remind them. If the supervisor responded NO, review their comments and take the required action.

**Can I track Service-Learning hours from a past experience?**
Yes. If you were enrolled at FGCU at the time of service and the experience meets Service-Learning guidelines, you may create a form to track those hours. A staff member from the agency must still serve as your supervisor and verify your hours. Contact the agency before creating your form to confirm your previous supervisor is still available; if not, ask who can verify your hours.

## Service-Learning Fact vs. Fiction

Organizations & Eligibility: Service-learning must benefit a registered nonprofit or government agency. Service for businesses, informal groups, individuals, or unregistered organizations does not qualify. The organization must be currently registered at the time of service. Always list the full legal name of the organization. You must list the nonprofit or government agency that directly benefited — not a student organization you are associated with. Homeowners Associations (HOAs) do not qualify.

Fraternities, Sororities, RSOs & Clubs: Only activities that directly benefit a nonprofit or government agency count; activities that benefit only the chapter or club do not. Holding an executive board position does not earn hours unless you document specific service for a qualifying nonprofit. Meetings, recruitment, fundraising for the chapter treasury, or internal planning do not meet the criteria.

Internships, Block Experiences & Teaching Roles: Hours cannot be double-counted — internship hours tracked in Eagle Career Network cannot also be tracked in Eagle Service Network. Hours exceeding required internship hours may be tracked for service-learning if they meet the requirements. For College of Education, only Block 1 and Block 2 may qualify (submitted in Eagle Service Network); Block 4 and 5 must be submitted in Eagle Career Network. To count, you must be an officially registered, approved non-stipend TA, LA, or CA.

Type of Work Performed: Shadowing alone does not qualify; you must perform active service. Donated items count only as the actual time spent (collecting, transporting, processing), not the quantity donated. Note-taking qualifies only if you are officially approved through FGCU Adaptive Services and list Adaptive Services as the organization served. Students can receive a maximum of ten hours total toward their requirement for animal fostering.

Form Details & Documentation: Even if you haven't started, you must describe what you expect to do. Your supervisor must be an employee of the nonprofit/government agency or an FGCU faculty or staff member, and the supervisor email must belong to the actual person verifying your hours.

Experiential Learning Types: Eagle Service Network is only for service-learning — internships, paid work, or other experiential learning cannot be documented there. You must select "Service-Learning" as the Experiential Learning Type for your hours to be reviewed correctly.
