Rodman Smith earned his Bachelor’s degree in Biology with a concentration in Biotechnology from Shippensburg University and a Master’s degree in Biotechnology with a concentration in Bioinformatics from The Johns Hopkins University. He joined FGCU in the summer of 2024 after 20 years with the AIDS and Cancer Virus Program (ACVP) at the Frederick National Laboratory for Cancer Research.
During his time with the ACVP, Rodman’s research focused on the large-scale production and purification of HIV and SIV using continuous flow sucrose density gradient ultracentrifugation. His expertise also includes developing methods for large-scale virus purification utilizing tangential flow filtration, as well as immunoassay development and optimization. Additional experience includes monoclonal antibody and recombinant protein production and purification.
Rodman draws on his strong background in cellular and molecular biology to create engaging, hands-on lab experiences that help students build practical skills, grow their confidence, and succeed in their academic and professional goals.
