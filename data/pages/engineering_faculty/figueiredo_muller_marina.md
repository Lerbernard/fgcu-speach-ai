Dr. Marina Figueiredo Muller is an Instructor II in the Department of Construction Management at Florida Gulf Coast University and an Assistant Professor on leave from the Pontifical Catholic University of Paraná (PUCPR), Brazil. She holds a Ph.D. in Systems and Production Engineering, with academic training and professional experience spanning construction management, engineering education, and digital technologies applied to the built environment.  
Dr. Muller’s research centers on Building Information Modeling (BIM) interoperability, sustainability, and the integration of emerging technologies—such as artificial intelligence and ontology-based data structures—into construction workflows. Her work addresses challenges related to data exchange, digital representation, and accessibility, including the development of BIM-based tactile maps and 3D-printed solutions to support individuals who are blind or visually impaired. She is particularly interested in how digital tools can enhance both construction practice and learning outcomes.  
In the classroom, Dr. Muller emphasizes experiential and project-based learning, incorporating BIM software, AI-supported tools, and real-world case studies into her courses. She teaches a range of undergraduate and graduate courses in construction management, BIM, construction graphics, sustainability, and construction methods, and actively integrates her research into teaching to prepare students for evolving industry demands.

**Research and Teaching Interests**
Dr. Muller’s research focuses on BIM interoperability, with emphasis on sustainability, accessibility, and data exchange across construction systems. Her work explores how emerging technologies—such as artificial intelligence and ontology-based data structuring—can improve BIM workflows and decision-making in construction and infrastructure projects.  
She actively integrates research into teaching through project-based learning, hands-on BIM assignments, games and group dynamics. Current educational research includes the use of AI-based BIM tools in the classroom and the development of tactile maps using BIM and 3D printing to support accessibility for individuals with visual impairments.
