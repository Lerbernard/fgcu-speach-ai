## Dhakal, Sudip (PhD)
**Assistant Professor, Software Engineering**
Department of Computing & Software Engineering
sdhakal@fgcu.edu
Dr. Sudip Dhakal is an Assistant Professor of Computing and Software Engineering at Florida Gulf Coast University. His research and teaching span across computer science, software engineering, and cybersecurity, with a particular focus on autonomous driving systems and self-driving vehicle technologies. Dr. Dhakal’s work has been published in leading journals and presented at top-tier conferences in the fields of intelligent transportation systems, computer vision, and cybersecurity. In the classroom, he is passionate about bridging theory and practice, integrating his cutting-edge research into hands-on, project-based learning experiences that prepare students to solve real-world technological challenges.

**Education**
    * Ph.D. in Computer Science and Engineering, University of North Texas
    * M.S. in Computer Science, University of North Texas
    * B.S. in Software Engineering, Pkhara University (Nepal)

**Specialties**
    * Self-driving vehicles
    * Autonomous driving systems

**Research and Teaching Interests**
Dr. Dhakal’s research focuses on object detection, cooperative perception, and security in autonomous driving systems, as well as data privacy and risk analysis in emerging technologies. He incorporates his research into teaching by engaging students in applied projects, such as developing secure software applications, implementing AI-driven perception models, and analyzing security risks in connected systems.
