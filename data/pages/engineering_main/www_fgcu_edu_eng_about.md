# U.A. Whitaker College of Engineering — About

FGCU's School of Engineering was established in fall 2005 and became the U.A. Whitaker College of Engineering (WCE) in August 2011. The college is located in Holmes Hall, specifically designed for student-centered teaching of engineering, computing, and construction in an active and collaborative learning environment.

The college has over 60 faculty and staff and over 1,200 students.

## Mission
Transform students in engineering, computing, and construction management into ethical and empowered graduates who lead with innovation, elevate their professions, strengthen their communities, and help shape the future of Southwest Florida and beyond.

## Vision
Lead a new era of engineering, computing, and construction management education and innovation by igniting individual potential, accelerating educational and societal transformation, and driving bold progress throughout Southwest Florida and far beyond.

## Value Proposition
The WCE offers high-quality programs in Engineering, Computing, and Construction through a student-centric ecosystem with experiential learning, dedicated faculty, program-specific accreditation, state-of-the-art classrooms and laboratories, and professional development opportunities. Graduates are equipped with the highest standards of disciplinary knowledge and a broad mindset to contribute meaningfully to their professions and communities.
